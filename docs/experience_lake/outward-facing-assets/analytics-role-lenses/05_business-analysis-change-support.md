## 1. What This Lens Means

`Business Analysis, Change, and Decision Support` means reading the platform as an operating environment that can be understood, shaped, and improved through structured analysis of:
- processes
- handoffs
- data use
- reporting logic
- decision needs
- operational impact

This lens is not mainly about:
- dashboards alone
- raw modelling
- platform engineering for its own sake

It is about:
- understanding how the operating world works
- defining what needs to change or improve
- translating between governed data reality and business-facing needs
- identifying impact, trade-offs, and requirements
- using structured analysis to support better decisions

So the person working through this lens becomes the one who helps make the environment:
- legible
- changeable
- explainable
- aligned between operational need and analytical/system reality

## 2. Why This Lens Fits This Platform World

This lens fits the platform because the world already contains:
- clear operating stages
- explicit truth surfaces
- event, case, and outcome flow
- analytical and reporting possibilities
- documented contracts and boundaries
- clear distinctions between what enters the world, how it becomes workflow, and how it becomes outcome truth

That means this is not an empty technical environment. It is a structured operating world with:
- process logic
- business questions
- decision points
- candidate KPI layers
- candidate reporting layers
- potential change surfaces

So the lens fits naturally because it can ask:
- what problem is this world trying to solve?
- how does information move into action?
- where do process, data, and decision requirements meet?
- what would need to be defined, improved, or clarified for the environment to work better?

## 3. Core Governed Data And Meaning Surfaces For This Lens

This lens would sit on top of both:
- the governed data world
- the structural meaning of the platform

That means it would use:
- event truth
- case chronology
- label truth
- campaign/context structure
- KPI and reporting candidates
- documented source and join posture
- flow from activity to case to authoritative outcome

The key difference here is that this lens is not only asking:
- what does the data say?

It is also asking:
- what process does this data represent?
- what decision does this output support?
- what requirement or business need sits behind this view?

## 4. What This Person Would Actually Do

Under this lens, the person would:
- analyse how the operating world behaves
- identify where information needs exist
- define reporting, KPI, or analytical requirements from stakeholder need
- assess the impact of changes to metrics, workflow interpretation, or reporting structure
- translate between governed data reality and business-facing decision needs
- help shape structured outputs that support better decisions

That can be expanded more concretely.

### 4.1 Analyse The Operating Process

This means:
- understanding the flow from event activity to suspicious pressure to case creation to authoritative outcomes
- understanding where in that chain decisions are made or could be improved

In this platform world, that would likely involve:
- mapping event-to-case flow
- mapping case-to-label progression
- identifying where pressure accumulates
- identifying where workflow or outcome quality weakens
- identifying what information is needed at each stage
- expressing those stages as concrete process maps and decision points rather than leaving them as narrative only

### 4.2 Define Information And Reporting Requirements

This means:
- deciding what operational stakeholders would need to see
- deciding what leadership would need summarised
- deciding what analytical users would need to drill into

In this platform world, that would likely involve:
- defining KPI families
- defining what should be reported by period, by campaign, or by workflow state
- deciding which reporting outputs support operational review versus strategic understanding
- turning platform truth into structured information requirements

More concretely, this would likely produce artefacts such as:
- a page-by-page reporting requirement map
- a stakeholder-to-view matrix
- KPI purpose notes tied to actual decision points
- drill-through requirement notes for anomaly and exception investigation

### 4.3 Assess Impact And Trade-Offs

This means:
- understanding how a change in process, metric definition, reporting view, or analytical logic would affect downstream interpretation and action

In this platform world, that would likely involve:
- assessing the effect of redefining a KPI
- assessing how a segmentation change affects prioritisation
- assessing the effect of changing which truth surface is used for a view
- assessing whether a new report or drill-through helps or confuses stakeholders

This should be understood as concrete analytical and reporting trade-off work, for example:
- whether event truth or case truth is the right basis for a given metric
- whether leadership needs summary stability while analysts need segment volatility exposed
- whether a change creates better actionability or just more noise

This lens is especially useful here because the platform already has enough governed structure to make such trade-offs meaningful.

### 4.4 Translate Platform And Data Reality Into Business-Facing Meaning

This means:
- not leaving the system at the level of technical truth surfaces
- helping operational or leadership audiences understand what those surfaces mean in business terms

In this platform world, that would likely involve:
- explaining what suspicious volume means for workflow burden
- explaining what case backlog means for service responsiveness
- explaining what accepted-label yield means for outcome quality
- translating concentration analysis into prioritisation logic

This is one of the strongest responsibilities in this lens because the platform already has clear movement from event truth to operational burden to authoritative outcome. The business-analysis task is to make that chain legible.

### 4.5 Support Change Through Structured Analysis

This means:
- identifying where the current information environment is insufficient
- proposing clearer structures, outputs, or logic
- helping define how an improved analytical or reporting layer should work

In this platform world, that would likely involve:
- identifying missing views in the reporting model
- identifying where stakeholders would need better drill-through
- defining clearer KPI ownership and interpretation
- shaping a more structured reporting or analytical product

The output here should be thought of as something tangible, such as:
- a proposed revised dashboard information architecture
- a refined KPI ownership map
- a recommended anomaly-review structure
- a proposal for separating executive, operational, and analytical views more cleanly

### 4.6 Challenge Assumptions And Clarify Requirements

This means:
- not accepting vague reporting or analysis requests at face value
- checking whether the request aligns with the actual governed world and decision need

In this platform world, that would likely involve:
- challenging whether a metric is actually meaningful
- checking whether the requested view is possible from the available truth surfaces
- clarifying the real stakeholder need behind a reporting request
- ensuring the final output reflects the actual question rather than a vague ask

This is also where the lens protects the environment from weak change requests that are visually attractive but analytically unhelpful.

## 5. What This Lens Would Examine

Typical subjects under this lens would include:
- process flow from event to case to outcome
- reporting and KPI definition needs
- decision points and decision gaps
- impact of metric or view changes
- stakeholder-oriented interpretation of operational truth
- differences between what is measurable and what is genuinely decision-useful

These can be organised into families:

### 5.1 Process Understanding

- workflow stage definitions
- case movement and handoff meaning
- concentration of burden in the operating flow
- relationship between pressure and operational action

### 5.2 Requirement Definition

- what needs to be reported
- what needs to be measured
- what needs explanation rather than just display
- what needs drill-through rather than summary

### 5.3 Impact And Change

- what changes if we redefine a KPI
- what changes if we use a different source
- what changes if we reshape a report or dashboard page
- what new visibility a proposed analytical change would create

### 5.4 Decision Support

- what information helps operations act
- what information helps leadership understand direction
- what information helps analysts investigate
- what information is noise rather than useful signal

## 7. What Questions This Lens Answers

This lens answers questions such as:
- what process is this platform actually representing?
- what decisions should the reporting or analytical layer support?
- what should be measured, and why?
- what is the business meaning of this governed truth?
- where are the information gaps?
- what would need to change to make this environment more useful to its consumers?

It also answers more practical questions such as:
- does this KPI actually serve a decision?
- does this report solve the real stakeholder need?
- what drill-through should exist beneath this summary?
- which process stage needs better visibility?
- which proposed change is meaningful versus cosmetic?

## 8. What It Would Look Like On This Platform Specifically

A practical first pass on this platform would likely be:

1. map the operating journey from event pressure to case activity to authoritative outcome
2. identify the main stakeholder decision points
3. define the KPI and reporting needs implied by those decision points
4. assess which views already exist in concept and which are missing
5. shape proposals for:
- dashboard structure
- KPI definitions
- drill-through needs
- anomaly review structure
- leadership summary structure
- process and decision maps showing where each output is intended to help

This would result in a business-analysis layer where:
- platform truth becomes easier to interpret
- reporting and analytics become more purpose-driven
- stakeholders have clearer visibility into what they need
- the analytical environment becomes easier to evolve intentionally
- proposed changes are easier to defend because they are tied to actual process, decision, and information-need analysis

## 9. Practical Tooling Expression

This lens would naturally be expressed through:
- structured requirement notes and process maps
- `SQL` and analytical-table understanding where requirement definition depends on actual data shape
- `Power BI` or equivalent BI structure where page, drill-through, or KPI needs are being designed
- documentation and comparison notes for options, impacts, and definitions
- summary packs for communicating proposed structures and meaning

The tool mention matters here because this lens is one of the clearest routes in the platform into:
- structured requirement shaping
- KPI definition support
- dashboard and reporting design logic
- process-aware analytical interpretation
- business-facing explanation of technical truth

## 10. What This Lens Unlocks In Practice

From this lens, the platform starts to support responsibility statements such as:
- analysed the operating flow from event intake through case and outcome stages to identify information and reporting needs
- defined KPI and reporting structures based on stakeholder decision requirements
- translated governed analytical truth into business-facing views and explanation
- assessed the impact of metric, view, and reporting-structure changes
- helped shape reporting and analytical outputs that better supported operational and leadership decisions
- challenged vague reporting asks and clarified the actual information need behind them

Again, the point is not the wording itself. The point is that these change and decision-support responsibilities become real and inspectable in this world.

## 11. Essence Of The Lens

`Business Analysis, Change, and Decision Support` turns the platform into a structured decision environment, and the person working through this lens becomes the one who helps define what needs to be seen, understood, changed, and supported for that environment to work better.
