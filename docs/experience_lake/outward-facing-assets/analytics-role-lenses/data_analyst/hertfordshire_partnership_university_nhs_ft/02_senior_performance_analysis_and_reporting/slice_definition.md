## 1. Requirement Being Targeted

The requirement being targeted here is the Hertfordshire Partnership University NHS Foundation Trust `Data and Information Officer` expectation around:
- analysing performance information to a high standard
- interpreting performance information to a high standard
- presenting performance information to a high standard
- developing and reporting performance targets from team level to senior managers
- producing reports, forecasts, or trend analysis that help senior managers understand current position, emerging risk, and required action

This maps directly to the Hertfordshire candidate-profile requirement that the analyst should be able to sound like someone who:
- is credible with senior audiences
- can explain not just current position but also trend and emerging risk
- can shape high-standard performance reports rather than only routine monitoring views
- can support senior decision-making with analytical interpretation, not just KPI display

This note exists because that requirement is easy to flatten into the vague statement:
`good at senior reporting`

That is not the real burden.

The employer is asking for something sharper:
- high-standard performance analysis
- concise senior-manager reporting
- trend interpretation
- bounded forward-looking or trajectory reading
- clearer management-useful implications

The current platform does not contain literal Adult Community Mental Health leadership packs, `SBU` governance cycles, or a real `NHS` forecasting function, so the slice has to use an honest analogue.

The closest truthful analogue is:
- one governed target-performance lane already established through Hertfordshire `A + D`
- one senior-facing summary pack built on that lane

- one bounded forward-looking or expected-direction reading that stays within the evidence

So for this slice:

The claim must therefore remain:
