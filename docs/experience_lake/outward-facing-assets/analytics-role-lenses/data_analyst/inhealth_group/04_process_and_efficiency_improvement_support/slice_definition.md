## 1. Requirement Being Targeted

The requirement being targeted here is the InHealth `Data Analyst` expectation around:
- using analysis to support process or efficiency improvement
- helping operational teams understand where effort, throughput, or quality is being lost
- turning identified patterns into bounded review or improvement-support recommendations
- making analytical outputs useful for operational follow-through rather than passive observation

This maps directly to the InHealth candidate-profile requirement that the analyst should be able to:
- support service and efficiency improvement with evidence
- move from reporting or pattern detection into something operationally useful
- help show where review or change attention should be focused
- contribute analytical support to process or performance improvement discussions

This note exists because that requirement is easy to overstate as:
`I improved the process`

That is not the burden here.

The employer is asking for something narrower and more defensible:
- identify where the analytical evidence points to avoidable effort or weaker efficiency
- translate that evidence into a bounded improvement-support reading
- make one realistic targeted review recommendation without claiming that the operational change has already been delivered

The current platform does not contain literal healthcare waiting-list operations or patient-contact workflows, so the slice has to use an honest analogue.

The closest truthful analogue is:
- the rolling monthly programme lane already established in InHealth `3.A`
- the trusted reporting-safe interpretation established in InHealth `3.C`
- the persistent `50_plus` pressure-versus-quality gap established in InHealth `3.D`

So for this slice:
- `process and efficiency improvement support` in the employer wording maps to `bounded operational review support` on the current platform
- the proof burden is:
  - take the identified recurring pattern
  - show what efficiency or effort implication it carries
  - frame a realistic targeted review recommendation
  - stop short of claiming the process was already fixed

The claim must therefore remain:
- healthcare-role shaped
- platform-truth bounded
- careful not to imply direct process redesign ownership where this slice only proves analytical support for targeted review
