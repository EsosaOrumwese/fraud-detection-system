## 1. Requirement Being Targeted

The requirement being targeted here is the Money and Pensions Service `Data Insight Analyst` expectation around:
- analysing structured data to identify patterns or gaps
- analysing unstructured or narrative-style evidence to identify patterns or gaps
- combining those evidence types into one stronger analytical reading
- working with customer voice, complaints, monitoring outputs, and operational measures together rather than treating numeric reporting as the full story

This maps directly to the candidate-profile requirement that the analyst should be able to sound like someone who:
- can move beyond classic metric-only reporting
- can combine narrative-style and numeric evidence
- can identify patterns that would not be visible from one evidence type alone
- can keep the combined reading controlled and decision-useful

This note exists because that requirement is easy to flatten into the vague statement:
`worked with qualitative and quantitative data`

That is not the real burden.

The employer is asking for something sharper:
- structured evidence analysis
- unstructured or narrative-style evidence analysis
- one combined analytical reading
- a clearer pattern or gap because both evidence types are used together

## 2. The Analogue On The Current Platform

The current platform does not contain a literal customer-feedback text store, complaint corpus, survey comment platform, or free-text analytics pipeline, so the slice has to use an honest analogue.

The closest truthful analogue is:
- one structured mixed-source reporting lane already established in Money and Pensions Service `3.B`
- one governed output surface already established in Money and Pensions Service `3.A`
- one compact narrative-style analytical surface derived from coded issue, finding, recommendation, or action-language outputs already proven elsewhere on the platform
- one combined insight pack that shows how the narrative-style surface sharpens the numeric interpretation

So for this slice:
- `structured data` maps to `the governed mixed-source reporting lane and its explicit numeric summary/detail outputs`
- `unstructured data` maps to `narrative-style or text-like analytical evidence surfaces whose meaning is carried through coded issue, action, finding, or support language rather than only numeric measures`
- `combined analysis` maps to `one compact pack that shows a pattern or gap more clearly when both surfaces are read together`

The claim must therefore remain:
- mixed-type shaped
- structured-plus-narrative shaped
- platform-truth bounded
- careful not to imply literal `NLP`, free-text mining, or a full qualitative-research estate where this slice only proves one bounded structured-plus-narrative analytical reading

## 3. Relationship To Money And Pensions Service `3.B` And `3.A`

This slice should build directly on Money and Pensions Service `3.B` and `3.A`, but it is not the same thing.

What Money and Pensions Service `3.B` already proved:
- several evidence streams could be aligned into one common reporting grain
- one dashboard-style summary and one supporting detail output could be produced from the same integrated logic
- one shared attention point remained visible across the combined pack

What Money and Pensions Service `3.A` already proved:
- the mixed-source output lane has explicit required fields and dimensions
- the same summary and detail outputs remain governed and release-safe

What Money and Pensions Service `3.D` now needs to add:
- one narrative-style evidence surface that is not just another numeric summary
- one combined analytical reading that depends on both numeric and narrative-style evidence
- one clearer pattern, gap, or priority than the structured reporting pack alone provided
- one bounded caveat on what the narrative-style analogue does and does not represent

So the correct posture is:
- reuse the governed reporting lane where honest
- add one new mixed-type proof object on top of it
- keep the work bounded rather than pretending to cover a literal text-analytics or customer-research estate

## 5. Chosen Bounded Slice

The cleanest bounded slice for this requirement is:

`one structured-plus-narrative analytical pack with one governed numeric base, one narrative-style evidence surface, and one combined insight output showing a clearer focus pattern`

In the fraud-platform analogue, that means:
- start from the Money and Pensions Service governed mixed-source reporting base

- align both surfaces around the same bounded focus question
- materialise one combined insight output
- write one caveat note that explains the boundary of the narrative-style analogue

The slice should answer one narrow question:

That means the slice should produce:
- one mixed-type analysis scope note
- one structured evidence output
- one narrative-style evidence output

- one caveat note

This slice is the best analogue for the Money and Pensions Service requirement because it proves:
