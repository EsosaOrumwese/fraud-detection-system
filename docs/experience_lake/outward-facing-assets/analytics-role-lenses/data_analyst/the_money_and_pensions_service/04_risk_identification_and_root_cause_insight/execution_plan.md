## 8. Likely-Driver Strategy

The likely-driver story has to stay honest.

The first-pass likely-driver strategy should answer:
- what exactly is the early warning signal?
- what is the strongest evidence-backed reason it is appearing?
- which part of that explanation is observed and which part is inferred?
- why does the explanation stop at likely-driver or root-cause-style language rather than singular deterministic causality?

Allowed likely-driver posture:
- one bounded cause chain
- one defended primary driver plus caveat
- one plural or conditional explanation if the evidence stays plural or conditional

Disallowed likely-driver posture:
- claiming certainty the evidence does not support
- asserting a deterministic single root cause when the pack only supports a probable explanation
- importing unrelated improvement rhetoric as fake explanation

Decision rule:
- every retained driver statement must earn its place in either:
  - early warning interpretation
  - bounded investigation
  - practical intervention implication
- if a driver statement does not support one of those purposes, drop it

## 9. Intervention-Implication Strategy

This is what makes the slice useful rather than merely descriptive.

The first-pass intervention layer should answer:
- what should a team pay attention to first?
- what should be reviewed before broader intervention?
- what should be remeasured before any stronger conclusion is made?

Required posture:
- protect the signal
- review the focused driver
- remeasure after the bounded check or change

Disallowed posture:
- claiming a completed improvement outcome
- claiming service redesign
- claiming operational ownership beyond analytical support
