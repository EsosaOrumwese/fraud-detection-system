## 1. Requirement Being Targeted

The requirement being targeted here is the Money and Pensions Service `Data Insight Analyst` expectation around:
- working across systems to transform multiple data sources into meaningful reporting and dashboards
- building and maintaining insight dashboards and reports from tools such as `Decision Focus`, `Qualtrics`, or equivalent platforms
- providing regular reporting and dashboards for internal and external stakeholders
- translating fragmented evidence into coherent decision-support views rather than reporting from a single operational system

This maps directly to the candidate-profile requirement that the analyst should be able to sound like someone who:
- can bring together different forms of evidence into coherent dashboards and reports
- can build reporting products that do not depend on a single source alone
- can translate fragmented evidence into meaningful reporting and visuals
- can serve several stakeholder audiences through governed reporting outputs

This note exists because that requirement is easy to flatten into the vague statement:
`good at dashboards`

That is not the real burden.

The employer is asking for something sharper:
- cross-source reporting
- dashboarding built from several evidence streams
- reporting products that stay coherent when the data is fragmented
- outputs that remain useful to different stakeholders without collapsing into source-by-source dumps

## 2. The Analogue On The Current Platform

The current platform does not contain a literal `CX&Q` stack, a `Qualtrics` plus `Decision Focus` reporting environment, or direct Money and Pensions Service service-partner reporting cycles, so the slice has to use an honest analogue.

The closest truthful analogue is:
- several governed compact evidence surfaces already available on the platform
- one bounded integrated reporting pack that combines those surfaces into a single reusable view
- one dashboard-style summary and one reporting-style detail output from that same combined logic
- one audience note showing how the same mixed-source view can support more than one stakeholder use without multiplying uncontrolled variants

So for this slice:
- `mixed-source dashboarding and reporting` maps to `one integrated reporting pack built from several compact governed evidence surfaces rather than one source alone`
- `Qualtrics`, `Decision Focus`, or equivalent platforms maps to `separate evidence channels that need to be normalised and repackaged into one analytical surface`
- `internal and external stakeholders` maps to `one compact mixed-source view with explicitly bounded audience-ready outputs rather than a full reporting estate`

The claim must therefore remain:
- mixed-source shaped
- dashboard-and-reporting shaped
- platform-truth bounded
- careful not to imply literal tool ownership, a real `CX&Q` system estate, or a wide customer-insight platform where this slice only proves one bounded mixed-source reporting pack
