## 1. Requirement Being Targeted

The requirement being targeted here is the HUC `Data Analyst` expectation around:
- communicating technical or analytical content in plain language
- tailoring that explanation to different audiences
- ensuring reporting and analysis supports operational delivery and strategic decisions
- moving beyond passive description into action-oriented explanation

This maps directly to the HUC candidate-profile requirement that the analyst should be able to describe:
- making complex findings understandable to non-technical audiences
- interpreting KPI movement and analytical outputs in operational terms
- connecting reporting outputs to real service decisions
- making analytical work understandable and actionable for service operators, leadership, commissioners, and NHS-facing stakeholders

This note exists because that requirement pair is broad enough to drift into general storytelling or general reporting, but narrow enough to support one bounded proof slice that can later become a truthful `X by Y by Z` claim.

The cleanest lens stack for this HUC `3E + 3F` requirement is:

**Primary**
- `08 - Stakeholder Translation, Communication, and Decision Influence`

- `01 - Operational Performance Analytics`

**Strong support**
- `02 - BI, Insight, and Reporting Analytics`

Why this stack fits:

`08` is the main owner because this requirement asks how complex findings become understandable to non-technical decision-makers, how KPI changes and analytical outputs are translated into operational meaning, and how reporting and explanation support action rather than passive observation. `08` owns:
- tailoring explanations by audience
- building narrative around KPI movement
- handling challenge
- connecting outputs to action and follow-through

`01` is the co-primary lens because communication here still needs operational substance underneath it. `01` owns:
- KPI logic
- workflow diagnosis
- trend explanation

`02` matters because the communication here is not only speech or prose. It is also the reporting product. `02` owns:
- dashboards, summary packs, and audience-specific views

- defining reporting needs
- shaping governed truth into usable reporting output

`05` is the fourth lens because it keeps the communication tied to real decision needs. `05` owns:
- defining reporting and KPI requirements from stakeholder need
- translating governed truth into business-facing meaning

So the practical reading is:
- `08` explains clearly and connects the issue to action
- `01` supplies the operational meaning
- `02` packages that meaning into a usable reporting surface
- `05` makes sure the output serves a real decision rather than passive observation

Responsibility order:
- `08 -> 01 -> 02 -> 05`

Best execution order:
- `05 -> 01 -> 02 -> 08`

That execution order matters because it first defines the decision need and audience, then builds the KPI and performance interpretation, then packages it into a reporting surface, and finally refines the narrative, explanation, challenge handling, and action framing for non-technical users.

## 3. Chosen Bounded Slice

The cleanest bounded slice for this HUC requirement is:

`one what-changed and what-to-do-next service-line performance briefing for a single operational issue`

Use one narrow issue only:

`a weekly performance briefing on one segment showing a real bounded service-line issue translated into an action-oriented recommendation for operations and leadership`

That issue can take a form such as:
- worsening suspicious-to-case conversion
- rising backlog
- pressure rising without downstream value
- one segment with disproportionate operational burden and weak outcome quality

Why this slice works:
- it proves the analyst can interpret KPI movement rather than dump numbers
- it proves the analyst can explain that movement in plain language to non-technical readers
- it proves the analyst can package the interpretation into a usable reporting surface
- it proves the analyst can connect the issue to an operational or strategic response

This slice should produce:
- one decision-question note
- one minimum process and KPI-purpose map
- one bounded KPI comparison layer
- one compact issue briefing pack
- one executive brief
- one operational action note
- one challenge-response note

## 4. Why This Slice Was Chosen

This slice was chosen because it gives direct room for:
- interpreting one real issue rather than describing communication ability abstractly
- translating KPI movement into plain-language operational meaning
- packaging the interpretation into a compact reporting surface
- showing what should happen next rather than stopping at description

It also avoids the wrong kinds of drift.

The intention is not to:
- build a broad dashboard estate
- repeat the earlier HUC reporting-ownership slice as another recurring pack exercise
- produce many issues at once
- turn the slice into generic storytelling with no decision consequence
- claim strategic decision-support maturity across the full service line

The intention is to prove one sharper statement:
- I can take a bounded service-line issue, explain what changed and why it matters, tailor that explanation to operations and leadership, and turn it into a concrete action-oriented briefing

## 5. Refinement On The Issue Choice

This refinement matters.

The issue used for this slice needs to be a real bounded issue from the HUC lane, not a hypothetical conversion-and-backlog story inserted because it sounds plausible.

That means:
- the issue should come from the governed HUC service-line outputs already created in the earlier slices
- the evidence should determine whether the issue is pressure, conversion, backlog, outcome quality, or a segment-specific burden problem
- the slice should show one explicit decision or follow-up recommendation grounded in the actual bounded evidence

So even if the briefing looks simple on the surface, the proof burden is:
- not “I can write a neat explanation”
- but “I can explain a real bounded issue accurately enough that an operator or leader could act on it without being misled”

## 7. Lens-by-Lens Execution Checklist

### 7.1 Lens 05 - Define the decision need first

`05` is what stops this from becoming nice storytelling with no purpose. It owns process understanding, KPI and reporting requirement definition, decision-support framing, and clarifying what decision a view is supposed to support.

Tasks:
1. Choose one audience pair:
- operations lead
- senior or leadership audience
2. Choose one decision question:
- example:
  - is the current backlog increase a pressure problem, a conversion problem, or both, and where should attention go first?
3. Map the minimum process:
- suspicious-event pressure
- case creation
- backlog accumulation
- downstream outcome or yield
4. Define only `3-4` KPI families:
- volume or pressure
- conversion
- backlog or aging
- outcome quality or yield
5. Write one reporting-purpose note:
- what this briefing is for
- what action it should support
- what is out of scope
6. Write one stakeholder-view matrix:
- what operations needs
- what leadership needs
- what can stay out of the first page

### 7.2 Lens 01 - Build the operational interpretation

`01` is the operational substance lens. It owns KPI logic, workflow diagnosis, trend explanation, bottleneck detection, and identifying where intervention is needed.

Tasks:
1. Build one bounded comparison:
- current week versus previous week
- one segment or campaign slice only
2. Create the KPI layer:
- suspicious-event volume
- suspicious-to-case conversion
- open or aged case count
- one downstream yield or outcome signal
3. Identify one clear issue:
- conversion weakened
- backlog rose
- issue is broad-based or isolated
- pressure rose without equivalent downstream value
4. Write one `what changed` note:
- where pressure increased
- where conversion weakened
- whether the issue is volume, throughput, or both
5. Write one `why it matters` note:
- what this means for service responsiveness
- what happens if no action is taken
- which segment needs attention first

### 7.3 Lens 02 - Package it into a usable reporting surface

`02` is the reporting-product lens. It owns page structure, summary-pack design, KPI presentation, audience layering, and turning operational truth into navigable reporting output.

Keep this to one compact issue pack with `2` pages only.

**Page 1 - Executive summary**
- `3-4` headline KPIs
- current versus prior movement
- one short `what changed` summary
- one short `what this means` box

**Page 2 - Operational drill-through**
- one segment comparison
- backlog or conversion detail
- one exception or anomaly note
- one simple action or monitoring recommendation

Tasks:
1. Define the reporting product:
- first page for leadership
- second page for operators or drill-through
2. Translate KPI logic into reporting logic:
- trend cards
- comparison table
- short commentary block
- drill-through view
3. Keep the metric definitions stable:
- same KPI meaning across both pages
4. Add page notes:
- what each page is for
- how it should be read
- what not to infer from it

### 7.4 Lens 08 - Turn it into explanation and action

`08` is the main communication-and-influence lens. It owns translating technical output into decision meaning, tailoring the story by audience, handling challenge, and connecting the output to action and follow-through.

Tasks:
1. Write one executive narrative:
- what changed
- why it matters now
- what should be monitored next
2. Write one operational action note:
- what operations should look at first
- which segment or queue needs attention
- what short-term follow-up is justified
3. Write one challenge-response note:
- why trust this KPI?
- is the issue isolated or systemic?
- what caveats apply?
- what would invalidate the recommendation?
4. Annotate the pack for non-technical users:
- plain-language titles
- simple explanation of terms
- no assumption that the reader knows the underlying data model
5. Make one recommendation explicit:
- monitor
- escalate
- investigate
- reprioritise
- review process change
