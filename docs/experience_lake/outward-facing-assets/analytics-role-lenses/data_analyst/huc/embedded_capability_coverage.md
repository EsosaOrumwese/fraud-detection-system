# Embedded Capability Coverage - HUC Data Analyst

As of `2026-04-03`

Purpose:
- record how the embedded HUC requirements around tooling, documentation clarity, and data-driven decision support are already evidenced across the stronger HUC responsibility slices
- avoid overstating these embedded requirements as standalone ownership families when the underlying HUC evidence treats them as supporting capability
- preserve the exact wording and proof posture that should later feed the HUC personal summary and main CV bullets

Truth boundary:
- this note is not a new execution slice
- this note does not claim a separate standalone programme was built for `G`, `H`, or `I`
- this note reuses evidence already produced in HUC slices `01` to `04`
- it therefore supports embedded capability claims, tooling visibility, documentation visibility, and decision-support positioning
- it does not support claiming a full enterprise BI estate, a broad technical-writing programme, or a wholesale organisational culture transformation

---

## 1. Why These Requirements Are Treated Differently

The HUC requirements grouped here are:
- `G`: practical Excel / Power BI / SQL capability
- `H`: accurate documentation, report-process clarity, report logic, mappings, and user guidance
- `I`: helping deepen a culture of data-driven decision-making

These are real requirements, but they are not the same kind of responsibility as:
- multi-source operational analysis
- owned recurring reporting
- anomaly detection and discrepancy handling
- issue-to-action stakeholder briefing

Those earlier responsibilities are ownership families.

`G`, `H`, and `I` are better treated as:
- embedded proof-of-tooling
- embedded proof-of-clarity and repeatability
- embedded proof-of-decision influence

That means the correct treatment is:
- keep them visible
- map them to the stronger HUC slices
- let them reinforce the main bullets and personal summary
- do not force them into separate heavyweight execution slices

## 5. Recommended CV Treatment

The right treatment for `G`, `H`, and `I` is:
- not standalone flagship responsibility bullets
- but not invisible either

They should appear in two places.

### 5.1 Personal summary layer

These embedded capabilities belong naturally in a HUC-style personal summary such as:

> Operational reporting analyst with experience turning multi-source service data into accurate, repeatable performance reporting and decision-support outputs. Strong in SQL and BI-style reporting, with practical Excel capability and a focus on clear KPI definitions, usable documentation, and explaining trends in plain language so operational and senior stakeholders can act with confidence. Known for improving trust in data and helping move discussions from intuition-led reporting to evidence-based service decisions.

This captures:
- `G` in `practical Excel capability`, `SQL`, and `BI-style reporting`
- `H` in `clear KPI definitions, usable documentation, repeatable performance reporting`
- `I` in `improving trust in data` and `moving discussions ... to evidence-based service decisions`

### 5.2 Main bullet layer

The stronger HUC bullets should then carry these capabilities naturally, for example:
- brought multiple operational datasets together into one governed reporting slice using SQL and repeatable analytical views
- owned recurring service-line reporting with stable KPI definitions, documented run controls, and clear reporting caveats for operational and leadership audiences
- highlighted and resolved a material conversion discrepancy before it distorted weekly reporting and decision-making
- communicated bounded service-line findings in plain language and turned them into a targeted operational recommendation for leadership and operations

That keeps the embedded capabilities visible without distorting them into fake standalone ownership claims.

## 6. Bottom Line

`G`, `H`, and `I` are covered.

They are covered best as:
- embedded tooling proof
- embedded documentation and reporting-clarity proof
- embedded data-driven decision-support outcome proof

They should therefore shape:
- the HUC personal summary
- the wording of the main HUC bullets
- the tools and environment line

They should not become:
- three extra heavyweight slices
- three standalone flagship bullets
- three overclaimed pseudo-programmes that the bounded evidence does not actually support
