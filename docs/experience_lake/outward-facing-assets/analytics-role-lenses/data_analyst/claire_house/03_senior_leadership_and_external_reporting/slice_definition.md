- producing reports for senior leadership
- producing reports for trustees or trustee-style oversight
- producing reports to support trusts, funders, bids, tenders, benchmarking, compliance, or regulatory-style needs
- shaping reporting outputs that can be trusted outside the immediate operational-reporting audience

This maps directly to the Claire House candidate-profile requirement that the analyst should be able to describe:
- producing higher-accountability reporting outputs
- tailoring reporting for leadership and external-style audiences
- using concise and controlled reporting to support decisions, submissions, and oversight
- packaging the same underlying data into outputs that are appropriate for audiences with different trust and accountability demands

This note exists because that requirement is easy to flatten into the vague statement:
`good at senior reporting`

That is not the real burden.

The employer is asking for something broader and more valuable:
- concise leadership-ready reporting
- controlled external-style reporting cuts
- audience-specific reporting discipline
- trust, caveat, and submission posture beyond standard internal dashboarding
