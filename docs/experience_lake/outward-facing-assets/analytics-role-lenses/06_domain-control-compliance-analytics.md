## 1. What This Lens Means

`Domain-Control and Compliance Analytics` means reading the platform as a governed world where analytical responsibility is shaped by:
- rules
- exceptions
- reconciliation
- auditability
- accountability

This lens is not about broad exploratory analytics.

It is about:
- checking whether the world is behaving within expected control rules
- identifying discrepancies and exceptions
- supporting accountable reporting
- making sure outputs can survive scrutiny

## 2. Why This Lens Fits This Platform World

Even though the strongest direct source here is the payroll ad, this platform world still supports the control shape because it already contains:
- governed truth products
- bounded joins
- case and outcome logic
- consistency requirements
- comparison surfaces that can reveal mismatches or anomalies

So the transferable value is not payroll-specific law. It is the responsibility shape of controlled analytical verification.

## 3. Core Governed Data Surfaces For This Lens

This lens would mainly sit over:
- `s4_event_labels_6B`
- `s4_flow_truth_labels_6B`
- `s4_case_timeline_6B`
- `s4_flow_bank_view_6B`
- selected bounded views derived from linked behavioural and outcome surfaces

## 4. What This Person Would Actually Do

Under this lens, the person would:
- reconcile expected and observed outputs
- identify exceptions, discrepancies, and control breaks
- prepare high-accountability reporting views
- support audit-style explanation of how outputs were produced
- highlight risks that affect trust, accuracy, or operational control

## 5. What This Lens Would Naturally Produce

This lens would naturally produce:
- reconciliation tables
- discrepancy reports
- control exception summaries
- audit-support explanations
- rule-bound reporting outputs

## 6. Practical Tooling Expression

This lens would naturally be expressed through:
- `SQL`
- controlled tabular reporting
- exception-focused summaries
- documented reconciliation logic

## 7. Essence Of The Lens

`Domain-Control and Compliance Analytics` turns the platform into a controlled verification environment, and the person working through this lens becomes the one who checks, reconciles, explains, and defends high-accountability analytical outputs.
