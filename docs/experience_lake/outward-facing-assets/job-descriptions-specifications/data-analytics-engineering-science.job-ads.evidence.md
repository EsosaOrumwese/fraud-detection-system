## Data Scientist - Midlands Partnership NHS Foundation Trust

Source:
- `NHS Jobs` - <https://www.jobs.nhs.uk/candidate/jobadvert/C9301-26-0451?searchFormType=main&keyword=data&language=en>

Role family:
- `Data Science`
- healthcare analytics / NHS / public sector
- strongly hybridized with `data engineering`, `population health analytics`, and `AI governance`

### Job Purpose Evidence

The employer frames this role as a data scientist position that sits at the intersection of advanced analytics, data engineering, enterprise data platform delivery, and Population Health Management (`PHM`). The post is expected to develop advanced analytical models, algorithms, and data-driven insight to support clinical, operational, and strategic decisions across the Trust.

The role is also expected to support the Trust's data platform and data quality strategy by producing data science and PHM solutions aligned to enterprise architecture, data quality frameworks, and digital transformation priorities. The position is not framed as pure experimentation. It is explicitly tied to production-ready, governed, enterprise-aligned analytics and AI delivery.

The employer also expects this person to help build internal data science capability, support innovation and research, provide technical and analytical leadership for PHM, oversee the Population Health Information Officer, and support the Trust's transition toward AI-enabled analytics with models that are production-ready, governed, and aligned with the enterprise data platform strategy.

### Responsibilities Evidence

#### 1. Advanced analytics, machine learning, and modelling

- Develop and deploy predictive, descriptive, and prescriptive models.
- Use `Python`, `R`, `SQL`, `Fabric`, and `Azure Databricks`.
- Apply machine learning and statistical methods to identify risk factors, population cohorts, and intervention opportunities.
- Build and maintain risk stratification, segmentation, and forecasting models.
- Use modelling to inform PHM programmes and service planning.
- Extract, clean, and transform large and complex datasets from clinical, operational, and corporate systems.
- Work with the Data Warehouse Lead and engineering team to improve data accessibility, quality, and automation.
- Ensure models, feature sets, and analytical pipelines are reproducible, version-controlled, and documented.
- Work to Trust engineering standards such as `Git` and `Azure DevOps`.
- Translate complex analytical outputs into clear and actionable insight for operational, clinical, and strategic stakeholders.

#### 2. Population Health Management (`PHM`)

- Lead analytical work supporting population health and health inequalities initiatives across the Trust and system partners.
- Ensure PHM models and datasets conform to national standards, NHS data models, and interoperability requirements.
- Use data from primary, secondary, community, and social care sources.
- Identify unmet need, inform care planning, and evaluate interventions.
- Collaborate with the Population Health Information Officer to ensure timely and accurate PHM dashboards, reports, and analytics.
- Develop linked data pipelines and analytical models that support `Integrated Care System (ICS)` priorities.
- Align work with the `NHS England PHM Development Framework`.
- Lead the use of linked datasets for population segmentation, pathway mapping, and outcome measurement.
- Ensure compliance with data standards and governance in PHM work.

#### 3. Data quality expectations

- Embed data quality by design into modelling, feature engineering, and analytical workflows.
- Maximise completeness, accuracy, and consistency of analytical outputs, especially where outputs inform operational or clinical decisions.
- Support national dataset assurance, with examples including `MHSDS` and `CSDS`.
- Ensure data science outputs align with validated and quality-assured data sources.
- Support implementation of the Trust's Data Quality Framework.
- Contribute analytical insight to data quality improvement initiatives, including root-cause analysis and pattern detection.
- Work with analytics and data platform teams to ensure modelling datasets meet agreed quality standards.
- Support and sustain enterprise-wide data quality frameworks including validation, reconciliation, and monitoring in warehouse and cloud environments.

#### 4. NHS dataset integration and engineering collaboration

- Design, build, and maintain secure, efficient, and automated data pipelines.
- Use `SQL`, `Python`, `Azure Data Factory`, and `Databricks`.
- Integrate and validate data from major NHS datasets including `MHSDS`, `CSDS`, `IAPT`, `SUS`, `RTT`, `ECDS`, `PLICS`, and the `National Cost Collection`.
- Apply `NHS Data Dictionary` definitions for consistency and accuracy.
- Document analytical datasets and transformations with lineage, assumptions, and limitations.
- Support continuity and auditability.
- Identify anomalies and data gaps as part of data quality improvement.
- Develop methods to integrate both structured and unstructured data.
- Explicitly includes structured SQL data plus unstructured data such as clinical notes, text, and free-form fields.

#### 5. Technology, AI, and innovation

- Ensure AI and machine learning solutions comply with Trust AI governance, ethical standards, explainability requirements, and model risk management principles.
- Design and deploy AI-ready datasets in environments such as `Azure`, `Fabric`, `Databricks`, and `Snowflake`.
- Integrate advanced analytics into `Power BI` dashboards for interactive exploration and decision support.
- Identify automation, predictive alerting, and workflow optimisation opportunities.
- Contribute to the Trust's AI governance and assurance framework.
- Ensure data science solutions are responsible, transparent, and ethical.

#### 6. People leadership and technical leadership

- Provide professional leadership, line management, and development for the Population Health Information Officer.
- Provide technical guidance, mentoring, and quality assurance to data engineers and developers.
- Contribute to skills development plans.
- Promote continuous improvement and innovation.

#### 7. Collaboration and communication

- Work with the Head of Analytics and Insights, Data Engineers, and Principal Analysts to ensure outputs integrate with Trust-wide reporting and dashboards.
- Collaborate with clinicians, operational managers, finance, and performance teams to co-design data science projects.
- Translate outputs into actionable improvement strategies.
- Escalate only the most complex, high-risk, or strategically sensitive modelling decisions to the Head of Data Platforms and Engineering.
- Build strong partnerships with `ICS` and local authority intelligence teams.
- Contribute to system-level PHM analytics and shared intelligence initiatives.
- Present analytical findings and complex technical information clearly to non-technical audiences.
- Use visualisations, reports, and storytelling to communicate findings.
- Represent the Trust in regional and national NHS data science and analytics networks.
- Ensure data science outputs align with the Trust's single version of the truth.

#### 8. Systems, platforms, and technical environment explicitly named

- Regular use of `Azure`
- Regular use of `Azure Machine Learning`
- Regular use of `Data Factory`
- Regular use of `Power BI`
- Regular use of `Fabric`
- Frequent use of `Python`

#### 9. Decision-making and professional judgement expectations

- Act as specialist lead for data science and PHM analytics.
- Design, test, and deploy complex models with minimal supervision.
- Interpret incomplete, complex, or ambiguous datasets to identify trends, risks, and improvement opportunities.
- Choose suitable modelling techniques and evaluation methods.
- Ensure fairness, accuracy, and reliability of outputs.
